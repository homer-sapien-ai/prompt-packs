# LinkedIn Content Prompt Pack

9 prompts for LinkedIn content. Copy into ChatGPT/Claude.

---

## 1. Personal Brand Intro Post

```
Write a LinkedIn introduction post for [your name], a [your title/role] at [company/organization] with [X] years of experience in [your industry/field].

Key points to include:
- A hook that stops the scroll in the first line
- Who you help and how (the transformation you deliver)
- 2-3 credibility markers (key achievements, roles, or credentials)
- A unique perspective or philosophy that defines your approach
- A clear call-to-action (follow, comment with X, DM, etc.)

Tone: [Professional but human / Vulnerable yet confident / Sharp and opinionated - choose one]

Style: First-person, conversational, no jargon. Short sentences. Around 300 words. Include 3-5 relevant hashtags at the end.
```

---

## 2. Behind-the-Scenes of a Project

```
Write a LinkedIn behind-the-scenes post about [project name], a [brief description of what you built/launched/created].

Structure:
1. The "before" - what problem or gap existed? Why did this project need to happen?
2. The messy middle - 2-3 real challenges, doubts, or pivots you faced (show the human side)
3. How you overcame the hardest part - one specific moment where something clicked or you got help
4. The result - concrete outcome, even if it's a work in progress
5. One insight others can apply to their own projects

Include specific details: real numbers, actual obstacles, sensory details that make it feel authentic.

Tone: Relatable, honest, not polished. Around 400 words. End with a question to drive comments.
```

## 3. Opinion / Polarizing Take

```
Write a LinkedIn post stating a strong, debatable opinion on [topic/industry trend].

The opinion: [State your actual take - what do you believe that others might disagree with?]

Requirements:
- Lead with the opinion in the first 2 lines (no "hot take incoming" filler)
- Explain your reasoning with 2-3 concrete examples or observations
- Acknowledge one valid counterargument before dismissing it
- Make it specific to [your industry/context], not generic advice
- Push readers to form their own view

Tone: Confident, provoke thought, not aggressive. Avoid hedging language. Around 300 words.

Note: This should be a genuine opinion you hold, not something performatively controversial.
```

## 4. Lessons Learned from Failure

```
Write a LinkedIn post about a failure or setback in [your career/project].

Include:
- The situation: What happened? Be specific. (e.g., "I launched a product no one bought," "I got passed over for promotion," "I burned out and quit")
- What you thought and felt in the moment (raw, honest)
- What you learned - the real lesson, not a sanitized moral
- What you did differently after (behavior change, mindset shift, new approach)
- One piece of advice for someone going through the same thing

Do NOT frame it as "failure is good" or toxic positivity. Failure sucks. Show the struggle.

Tone: Vulnerable, reflective, not inspirational-essay style. Around 350 words. No humble bragging.
```

## 5. Tool / Resource Recommendation

```
Write a LinkedIn post recommending [tool/resource name] for [target audience].

Include:
- What problem it solves (paint the before state)
- How it works - 2-3 specific features that make the difference
- Your personal experience using it - one specific workflow or result
- Who it's NOT for (honest about limitations)
- A special angle or unconventional way you use it that others might not know

Do NOT write a generic feature list. Write a story about how [tool] changed something for you.

Tone: Genuine enthusiasm, practical, not salesy. Around 250 words. Include relevant hashtags.
```

## 6. Career Milestone Celebration

```
Write a LinkedIn post celebrating [milestone: e.g., 10 years at company / promotion / launching a new role / hitting a revenue target / X clients served].

Structure:
- Acknowledge the milestone directly (lead with it)
- Name the people who made it possible - specific shoutouts
- One unexpected lesson from the journey
- What you're most proud of (beyond the metric)
- What's next / what's exciting you now
- Gratitude that's specific, not generic "thank you all"

Do NOT make it braggy. Make it relational. The milestone is the frame; the people and meaning are the content.

Tone: Grateful, warm, forward-looking. Around 300 words.
```

## 7. Customer Success Story

```
Write a LinkedIn case study post about [client/company name] and the results you/your company delivered for them.

Include:
- The client's initial challenge (specific pain point, the "before")
- What you did together - the approach, not just the service
- The measurable results (use real numbers: revenue increase, time saved, conversion lift, etc.)
- One quote from the client (or create a realistic one that captures their experience)
- Why this case matters to you / what it taught you

Structure it as a story, not a press release. Weave in the results naturally.

Tone: Proud but not boastful, client-focused. Around 350 words. Use their name with permission.
```

## 8. How-To / Tutorial Post

```
Write a LinkedIn how-to post teaching readers how to [specific action/outcome].

Requirements:
- Hook: Start with a result or transformation (e.g., "I used to spend 5 hours on X. Now it takes me 20 minutes.")
- Problem: Why this is harder than it seems / common mistake people make
- Step-by-step process: Numbered steps, each actionable and specific
- Pro tip or nuance most people miss
- Call-to-action: Ask a question to drive engagement ("What's your biggest challenge with X?")

Make it genuinely useful - if someone followed these steps, would they get the result?

Tone: Generous, clear, confident. Around 400 words. Use formatting (line breaks, bold) in the final output.
```

---

## 9. Year in Review / Reflection

```
Write a LinkedIn year-in-review post for [year].

Structure:
- 3-4 high-impact moments or milestones from the year (specific, not generic "growth")
- The hardest part of the year (real talk)
- One thing you're most grateful for
- One thing you'd do differently (without beating yourself up)
- What you're carrying into [next year] - intention or theme
- Nudge readers to reflect on their own year

Include 2-3 specific numbers or moments that made it meaningful. Don't just list achievements - show what they meant.

Tone: Reflective, honest, not performatively vulnerable. Around 400 words.
```
