# Cold Outreach Prompt Pack

9 high-quality prompts for cold outreach. Copy into ChatGPT/Claude, fill in brackets.

---

## 1. Personalized Cold Email to Potential Client

```
Write a personalized cold outreach email for a sales context.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My company: [YOUR COMPANY]
- What I sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE DESCRIPTION]
- Prospect name: [PROSPECT NAME]
- Prospect company: [COMPANY NAME]
- One specific pain point or challenge I identified about their business: [PAIN POINT]
- One relevant achievement or fact about their company: [COMPANY FACTOR]
- Desired outcome: [MEETING/CALL/DEMO]

Write in a conversational, professional tone — not salesy or pushy. Keep it short (under 150 words). Include a specific, low-commitment call-to-action. Make the opening line personalized to them, not generic.
```

---

## 2. Follow-Up After No Response

```
Write a follow-up email for a cold outreach sequence.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- Prospect name: [PROSPECT NAME]
- Original email sent: [DATE or "a few days ago"]
- What I offered in the original email: [OFFER/SOLUTION]
- One new piece of value or insight to add: [NEW VALUE ADD]
- Desired outcome: [REPLY/MEETING/CALL]

Write a short, low-pressure follow-up (under 100 words). Reference the original email briefly. Don't apologize for reaching out again. End with a soft question or CTA. Keep the tone friendly but confident.
```

## 3. LinkedIn Connection Request

```
Write a LinkedIn connection request message.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My title/role: [YOUR TITLE]
- Reason for connecting: [MUTUAL INTEREST, COMPANY, POST THEY ENGAGED WITH, ETC.]
- Prospect name: [PROSPECT NAME]
- Prospect's role: [THEIR TITLE]
- One specific reason this connection makes sense: [RELEVANCE]

Write under 300 characters (LinkedIn's sweet spot). Be specific about why you're reaching out. No pitch — just a genuine connection request. Friendly, concise, natural.
```

## 4. Cold DM for SaaS

```
Write a cold DM (Twitter/DM) for a SaaS product.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- Product name: [PRODUCT]
- Key problem it solves: [PROBLEM]
- One key feature or differentiator: [FEATURE]
- Prospect name: [PROSPECT NAME]
- Their current tool or approach (if known): [CURRENT TOOL]
- Desired action: [SIGN UP FOR TRIAL/DEMO/REPLY]

Write in a short, punchy DM (under 150 words). Lead with a relevant observation about them or their work. Mention social proof if available ([NUMBER] users, [CUSTOMER TYPE] like them, etc.). Include one specific next step. Don't over-explain — spark curiosity, then pivot to conversation.
```

## 5. Referral Request Message

```
Write a warm referral request message to an existing connection.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- How I know them: [WHERE WE MET / WORKED TOGETHER / CLIENT RELATIONSHIP]
- Person I'm looking to be introduced to: [TARGET NAME]
- Target's role/company: [ROLE & COMPANY]
- Why this person would benefit from knowing me: [VALUE PROP]
- Favor I'm asking: [ASK — e.g., "a quick intro email," "a warm forward"]

Write in a warm, casual tone. Remind them of your connection or past interaction. Be specific about who the person is and why an intro would help. Make the ask crystal clear but low-effort for them. Offer to make it easy for them (provide an intro blurb they can copy-paste).
```

## 6. Job Outreach

```
Write a cold outreach email to a company about a job opportunity.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- Role I'm interested in: [JOB TITLE]
- Company name: [COMPANY]
- One specific thing I admire about the company: [ADMIRATION]
- My most relevant experience or achievement: [EXPERIENCE]
- One relevant skill or credential: [SKILL]
- Why I'd be a great fit: [FIT REASON]

Write a short, confident email (under 200 words). Lead with why you're reaching out to *them* specifically — not "I'm looking for a job." Show you've done your homework. Include your most relevant proof points. End with a specific, respectful ask (e.g., "I'd love to chat for 15 minutes").
```

## 7. Guest Post Request

```
Write a guest post outreach email to a blog owner or editor.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My site/platform (if applicable): [MY SITE]
- Guest post topic: [TOPIC]
- Why this topic fits their audience: [RELEVANCE]
- Brief credentials/expertise on the topic: [CREDENTIALS]
- Publication name: [PUBLICATION]
- One article from their site I can reference: [ARTICLE]

Write a concise, respectful pitch (under 200 words). Compliment something specific about their publication. Propose one clear topic with a brief angle. Show you understand their audience. Offer 1-2 headlines or angle options. Include links to previous work if relevant.
```

## 8. Partnership Proposal

```
Write a partnership outreach email.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- My company: [MY COMPANY]
- What we do: [DESCRIPTION]
- Their company: [PARTNER COMPANY]
- What they do: [DESCRIPTION]
- Specific partnership idea: [PARTNERSHIP TYPE — e.g., co-marketing, referral, integration, joint product]
- Mutual benefit: [WHY THIS HELPS BOTH]
- One thing they've done recently I can reference: [RECENT NEWS/POST/LAUNCH]

Write a professional, concise email (under 250 words). Open with something specific about them — not generic flattery. State the partnership idea clearly. Explain the mutual benefit. Make the next step easy. Suggest a specific call-to-action (e.g., "A 15-minute call to explore this?").
```

## 9. Icebreaker for Warm Intro

```
Write a short, natural icebreaker message for a warm introduction.

Context:
- My name: [YOUR NAME]
- The mutual contact introducing us: [MUTUAL CONTACT NAME]
- How the mutual contact described me to them: [CONTEXT]
- One reason this intro makes sense: [WHY]
- Brief note about what I do: [ONE-LINE PITCH]
- Desired outcome: [REPLY / CALL / MEETING]

Write a warm, brief message (under 100 words) that a mutual contact could forward or that could follow a warm handoff. Be natural — not salesy. Reference the mutual connection. Briefly explain who you are and why you're reaching out. Make the ask easy.
```
