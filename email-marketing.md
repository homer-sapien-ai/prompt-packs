# Email Marketing Prompt Pack

9 prompts for email marketing. Copy into ChatGPT/Claude.

---

## 1. Welcome Sequence Email

```
Write a warm, engaging welcome email for a new subscriber to [PRODUCT/SERVICE].

Context:
- Company name: [NAME]
- What they sell: [DESCRIPTION]
- Primary value proposition: [KEY BENEFIT]
- What new subscribers should expect: [FREQUENCY/TYPE OF CONTENT]
- Any onboarding offer or lead magnet they received: [DETAILS]

Write in a conversational tone that feels like a personal message, not a corporate email. Include:
- A strong opening that acknowledges their signup and expresses genuine excitement
- Brief introduction to who you are and what makes your [product/service] different
- What to expect going forward (email frequency, type of content)
- A clear call-to-action to take the next step [SPECIFIC ACTION]
- Friendly sign-off with a personal touch

Keep it under 200 words. No corporate jargon. Sound like a knowledgeable friend who wants to help.
```

## 2. Re-Engagement Email (Win-Back)

```
Write a re-engagement email to win back inactive subscribers who haven't engaged with our emails in [X WEEKS/MONTHS].

Context:
- Company: [NAME]
- What they sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE]
- Why subscribers might have gone inactive: [REASONS]
- What's new with the company: [UPDATES/IMPROVEMENTS]
- Incentive (if any): [OFFER/DISCOUNT]

Goals:
- Remind them who you are and what value you provide
- Acknowledge they've been gone without being accusatory
- Highlight what's new, improved, or worth coming back for
- Make it easy to re-engage with a low-friction CTA
- Give them an easy way to stay on the list or cleanly opt-out

Write in a warm, non-pressuring tone. Use "you" frequently. Include a compelling subject line. Keep it under 150 words.
```

## 3. Product Launch Announcement

```
Write an exciting product launch announcement email.

Context:
- Company: [NAME]
- Product name: [NAME]
- What it is: [DESCRIPTION]
- Key features/benefits (3-5 bullet points): [LIST]
- What problem it solves: [PAIN POINT]
- Launch date: [DATE]
- Launch offer/price: [DETAILS]
- Link to: [URL]

Structure:
- Hook: Start with something intriguing or surprising related to the launch
- Build: Briefly introduce the product and what makes it special
- Detail: 3-4 key features or benefits
- Offer: Launch pricing or incentive
- Urgency: Why they should act now (limited time, limited quantity, etc.)
- CTA: Clear button to [SHOP/SIGN UP/LEARN MORE]

Tone: Energetic but not spammy. Confident but not arrogant. Make it feel like you're sharing something genuinely exciting.
```

## 4. Customer Story / Case Study

```
Write a customer success story email featuring one of your clients.

Context:
- Customer name (or first name + company): [NAME]
- Their industry/role: [DETAILS]
- The challenge they faced before using [PRODUCT]: [PROBLEM]
- How they used your product/service: [USE CASE]
- The results they achieved (specific numbers if possible): [METRICS]
- A quote from the customer: [QUOTE]

Write as a compelling narrative (not a sales pitch) that:
- Opens with a hook or problem statement
- Briefly sets up who the customer is
- Shows their journey from problem to solution to results
- Includes a powerful quote
- Ends with a natural CTA related to the story

Tone: Authentic, storytelling-driven, credible. Make the reader think "that could be me" or "I want those results too."
```

## 5. Cart Abandonment Email

```
Write a friendly, effective cart abandonment recovery email.

Context:
- Company name: [NAME]
- Product(s) left in cart: [PRODUCTS]
- Cart value: [AMOUNT]
- What makes these products valuable/urgent: [REASON TO BUY]
- Any incentive you're offering: [DISCOUNT/FREE SHIPPING]
- Link to cart: [URL]

Goals:
- Remind them what they left behind (make it visual if possible)
- Address common objections or reasons for abandonment
- Create mild urgency without being pushy
- Make it easy to complete the purchase
- If offering a discount, include it naturally

Tone: Helpful, understanding, not desperate. Like a friendly store associate saying "hey, you left this behind." Keep it focused—single CTA.
```

## 6. Newsletter Opt-In Confirmation (Double Opt-In)

```
Write a confirmation email for someone who just signed up for your newsletter.

Context:
- Company/brand: [NAME]
- What the newsletter covers: [TOPICS]
- What they'll receive (frequency, type of content): [DETAILS]
- Any welcome bonus or lead magnet: [OFFER]
- Confirmation link: [URL]

Requirements:
- Confirm their subscription request
- Explain what they just signed up for
- Make the confirmation action clear and easy
- Optionally include a welcome teaser or bonus
- Set expectations for frequency and content type

Tone: Professional but warm. This is a transactional email—get them to click the button, but also start building the relationship.
```

## 7. Upsell / Cross-Sell Email

```
Write a targeted upsell/cross-sell email to existing customers.

Context:
- Customer's recent purchase: [PRODUCT]
- Product(s) you're upselling/cross-selling: [PRODUCTS]
- Why it's a good fit for them: [REASON]
- The relationship between current and suggested product: [HOW THEY COMPLEMENT]
- Offer/incentive (if any): [DISCOUNT/BONUS]
- Link: [URL]

Strategy:
- Reference their recent purchase naturally (show you remember them)
- Explain why [NEW PRODUCT] pairs well with what they bought
- Highlight the specific benefit they'd get
- If offering an incentive, make it feel like exclusive access or a thank-you
- Include one clear CTA

Tone: Helpful advisor, not pushy salesperson. You genuinely think this will help them—write like it.
```

## 8. Survey / Request for Feedback

```
Write an email asking customers for feedback on [PRODUCT/EXPERIENCE].

Context:
- What you're asking feedback on: [PRODUCT/RECENT EXPERIENCE]
- Why their feedback matters: [HOW IT WILL BE USED]
- How long the survey takes: [TIME]
- Incentive for completing (if any): [REWARD]
- Link to survey: [URL]

Goals:
- Make them feel valued and heard
- Explain why their input matters
- Lower barriers to participation (keep it short, make it easy)
- If offering a reward, present it as a thank-you, not a bribe

Tone: Appreciative, genuine, not demanding. Avoid "we want your opinion" clichés. Try "help us build something better" or "you're the expert here—we need your take."
```

## 9. Affiliate / Partner Promotion

```
Write a promotional email for an affiliate or partner product.

Context:
- Your relationship: [WHY YOU'RE RECOMMENDING THIS]
- Partner/affiliate: [NAME]
- Product: [NAME]
- What it does: [DESCRIPTION]
- Why you recommend it: [SPECIFIC REASON]
- Your experience with it (if applicable): [YOUR TAKE]
- Special offer/bonus for your audience: [DETAILS]
- Link: [URL]

Requirements:
- Be transparent that this is a paid/partnership recommendation
- Focus on genuine value, not just the sale
- Make it clear why *this* product/partner specifically
- Include the CTA with your unique link or bonus

Tone: Honest advocate, not salesperson. You recommending something because you genuinely think it will help—write like you're telling a friend.
```
